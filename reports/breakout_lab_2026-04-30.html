📖 How to read this report (click to collapse)

The breakouts table — what each column means

  • Levels (H S C) — which type of resistance broke today. H = High-Volume Node (where shares accumulated historically). S = 20-day swing high (recent peak). C = 52-week cycle high (yearly peak). More letters = stronger signal. "H+S" means both broke simultaneously.
  • Lvl ₹ — the actual price level that broke.
  • Vol× — today's volume / 20-day average. 2.0× = double normal volume; higher = more conviction.
  • CIR% — "Close-In-Range" — where today's close was in the day's high-low range. 100% = closed at day's high (very strong); 50% = middle; 0% = weak.
  • 50d / 200d — ✓ = stock above its 50-day / 200-day moving average (uptrend); ✗ = below (downtrend).
  • Score — composite 0-100 combining all of the above. ≥70 strong, 30-69 moderate.

The featured stock cards — three things to read

  1. The price chart (left) shows recent action. The horizontal lines are POC (orange = where most volume traded), VAH/VAL (green dashed = the 70%-volume range around POC). When price breaks above POC, often runs further; when it falls below, often retests POC as new resistance.
  2. The volume profile (right) is a sideways histogram of WHERE shares traded. Long green bars = areas where lots of shares changed hands = likely future support/resistance.
  3. The yellow "Disclosed" banner is the honest deals label. We only count the trades NSE publicly disclosed (bulk + block deals > size threshold). Most of every stock's volume is anonymous — no one knows who bought it. Anyone claiming to know per-stock FII flow is making it up.

What this report is NOT

It's NOT a buy list. The composite score tells you "this setup historically hit X% of the time on past data" — useful as research context, NOT a recommendation.
